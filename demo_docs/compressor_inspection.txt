INSPECTION DATE: 2026-04-15

COMPRESSOR C-201 — INSPECTION REPORT

1. OVERVIEW
Inspection conducted following repeated impeller fouling events between January and April 2026.
Total unplanned downtime attributed to C-201 fouling: 34 hours over 4 months.

2. IMPELLER CONDITION
Impeller found with heavy deposits on blade leading edges. Deposit composition: process fluid residue
and particulate matter. Blade erosion estimated at 8% of original thickness at trailing edges.
Efficiency loss estimated at 12% compared to baseline commissioning data.

3. SUCTION VALVE — VALVE-7
Valve-7 found with partial obstruction caused by accumulated particulate buildup.
Flow restriction estimated at 20% of rated capacity. Valve internals worn; recommend replacement
at next planned shutdown (target: Q3 2026).

4. UPSTREAM KNOCKOUT DRUM
Root cause investigation confirmed that the upstream knockout drum (KD-04) drain valve
(XV-12) was operating with sluggish actuation, allowing liquid carryover into C-201 suction.
Recommendation: replace XV-12 actuator. Priority: HIGH.

5. MOTOR AND BEARINGS
Motor winding insulation resistance: 420 MΩ (acceptable, >100 MΩ).
Bearing temperatures during steady-state operation: Drive-end 58°C, Non-drive-end 54°C (normal).
Vibration at 3.1 mm/s RMS (within acceptable limit of 7.1 mm/s).

6. RECOMMENDATIONS
- Replace XV-12 actuator on KD-04 knockout drum: HIGH priority
- Schedule Valve-7 replacement: Q3 2026
- Install inline suction strainer upstream of C-201: MEDIUM priority
- Increase impeller cleaning frequency from quarterly to bi-monthly until root cause resolved

7. RETURN TO SERVICE
C-201 cleared for return to service following impeller cleaning and Valve-7 restoration.
Post-maintenance vibration: 3.1 mm/s RMS. Discharge pressure: 7.4 bar. Motor current: 39A.
