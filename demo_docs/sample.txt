Pump P-101 experienced seal leakage after 500 operating hours. The leakage was first detected on 2023-10-15. During inspection, abnormal vibration (0.5 mm/s) was also noted, which is above the acceptable limit of 0.2 mm/s. The pump was taken offline for maintenance on 2023-10-16. Bearing wear was observed during inspection. The root cause analysis indicated that the lubricant viscosity was incorrect for the operating temperature. SOP-04 specifies the use of ISO VG 46 oil for this pump, but ISO VG 68 was used. SOP-23 states that lubrication should be performed every 500 operating hours; the last lubrication was 480 hours ago.

Maintenance Actions Performed:
1. Replaced shaft seal (Part No. 12345)
2. Replaced bearing assembly (Part No. 67890)
3. Cleaned pump casing
4. Refilled with correct lubricant (ISO VG 46)
5. Performed alignment check (alignment within tolerance)

Findings:
- The previous lubricant (ISO VG 68) was causing excessive heat generation.
- Bearing wear was likely accelerated due to high operating temperatures.
- Vibration levels dropped to 0.15 mm/s after maintenance.

Recommendations:
1. Update maintenance checklist to include lubricant viscosity verification.
2. Implement vibration monitoring for critical pumps.
3. Conduct follow-up inspection after 100 operating hours.
